BEFORE THE EXPERIMENT · SYNTHETIC EVIDENCE · 20 JUL 2026

Before you commit tens of millions of dollars to predict evolution, find out whether the experiment can teach you enough.

A research director deciding whether to fund a large evolution experiment faces a question that money cannot answer after the design is locked. Will the study separate a good forecast from a lucky guess, or spend tens of millions of dollars and leave the same uncertainty behind? EvoForecast tests that decision first in software. Its registered simulation engine runs the proposed experiment in a synthetic world, where the answer is known, then checks whether each design could recover it before any real programme begins.

THE COSTLY FAILURE · EXTERNAL CONTEXT

A programme can spend more and still not tell two forecasts apart.

Every large evolution experiment locks in choices that are costly to reverse. Those choices include species, generations, independent populations, starting diversity, conditions, measurements, and automation. More spending cannot guarantee that one forecast will be distinguishable from another. An experiment that leaves that comparison unresolved has failed its central decision, even if every measurement succeeds. That is the costly failure to prevent.

WHY AVERAGES MISLEAD · PROSPECTIVE DISCIPLINE

A better average can still hide a forecast that misses too often.

The model ladder compares tracks with progressively richer information. Across its 35 synthetic contexts, the genome-plus-phenome track, combining genome data with measured traits, improved mean forecast accuracy by 47.5 percent over the pedigree baseline based on family lineage. This aggregate is separate from every single design cell. Its uncertainty range was still too narrow. A ninety percent prediction range should contain the truth nine times in ten. This one held it eight. Across the sixty prospective, or forward-looking, cells, no design cleared accuracy and calibrated uncertainty together. A held-out outcome is one withheld until scoring, so the test cannot reward an explanation made after the result is known.

THE REHEARSAL · AUDITABLE CHAIN

Rehearse the whole experiment in a world where the answer is already known.

EvoForecast builds a synthetic population and advances it with SLiM 5.2, the registered trajectory engine for this round. The surrounding challenge software, or harness, is engine-agnostic, which means it can work with more than one trajectory engine. Other evolutionary simulators are under evaluation. Later rounds aim to develop ecological network dynamics in a population context and test feedback between ecological change and evolution. This round does not establish that capability. For each synthetic run, SLiM records the true outcome and the harness keeps it hidden while releasing only what a real study could measure. Competing designs deposit their forecasts before reveal. The harness then compares what each design learned, what resources it used, and how it failed.

V3 · THE POSITIVE CONTROL

First, prove the instrument can find a signal that is really there.

V3, V4, and V5 are successive challenge versions. Each starts by testing whether the software can recover a signal planted on purpose. In V3, the favorable control tied the signal to a known genetic marker. A forecaster allowed to use genome data improved the score by 17.0 percent over one limited to family lineage, across sixteen matched sets of runs. When the matched control removed that signal, the same comparison fell by 9.2 percent. These controls show that the workflow can recover a favorable software signal and can also return an adverse result. They do not measure the value of DNA in a living animal.

V4 · THE EVIDENCE WE THREW OUT

Next, throw out the evidence that cannot be trusted.

V4 looked encouraging and was rejected. Independent review found three faults. The release exposed information that should have remained hidden. The paired comparisons no longer matched the analysis promised in advance. The cost estimates disagreed with the stated consequences of forecast error. None of the V4 estimates were carried forward. Keeping a flattering result after its test has broken would make the instrument unsafe for decisions. The rejection shows that review can stop bad evidence before it shapes a programme.

V5 · THE SCALE-UP DECISION

The clean run that said do not scale yet.

V5 was the version to act on because every forecast was locked before reveal. All 5,600 synthetic runs finished without failure. The genome-plus-phenome track still improved the average model-ladder score, while the decision test required accuracy and calibrated uncertainty together. Its stored probability of meeting that joint definition had to reach fifty percent, making a pass more likely than not. Only one of eighty design cells crossed that line. Each cell combines one information track, population count, and forecast horizon. The sole passing cell was a day-90 nowcast at 52.5 percent. A nowcast estimates the outcome at the end of the same observation window, so it is not prospective evidence. Among the sixty forward-looking cells, zero reached fifty percent. Zero of eighty cells reached the robust eighty percent bar. Zero of one hundred twenty experiment plans were robustly best. The feedback track added 0.0 percent beyond genome plus phenome. The instruction is clear. Do not scale a molecular forecasting programme on this synthetic evidence yet.

WHAT WE CAN SAY NOW · CLAIM LADDER

The software is proven here. The biology still needs a real test.

The evidence reaches the second rung and stops there. Reproducible execution and source records checked by digital fingerprints support the software rung. These fingerprints, called hashes, show when a source file has changed. The synthetic challenges provide conditional evidence about experiment design. Real material, qualified laboratory measurements, custody, an independent reveal, and outside reproduction are still required before any empirical or operational claim. A finished report cannot move a claim upward. New evidence can.

WHAT A REAL EXPERIMENT MUST SHOW · PROPOSED / BLOCKED

The hard part a real study still has to prove.

The proposed next design would model diploid organisms, with two copies of each of ten chromosomes, sexual reproduction, and only the genetic variants already present in the founders. It would connect founder genomes, the reshuffling of parental DNA called recombination, measurable traits, ecology, measurement error, and blind scoring. Later work aims to add ecological network dynamics around populations and test feedback between ecological change and evolution. Those capabilities are proposed, unbuilt, and uncalibrated. This cutaway marks where real material, laboratory measurements, and independent review must replace software assumptions.

ONE BOUNDED NEXT STEP

Run one small round together before anyone scales.

Bring one candidate keystone organism whose effects shape its community, one feasible budget stated in USD, and one outcome that stays hidden until scoring. We rehearse the design in the wind tunnel, then run the smallest real round that can estimate calibration and forecast accuracy under an independent reveal. After that round clears every gate, later rounds can add interacting species around the focal organism, then genetic backgrounds, controlled conditions, spatial structure, and small model ecosystems. Molecular measurements expand last.
